Accountability means taking responsibility for their actions, decisions, and the outcomes of their research. It involves being transparent about their methods, data, and findings, as well as acknowledging and rectifying any mistakes or errors. Scientists and researchers are accountable to their peers, the scientific community, and the public to ensure the integrity and credibility of their work. By upholding accountability, they contribute to the trustworthiness of scientific research and its potential impact on society.
